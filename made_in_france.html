Made in France

From the idea to your feet, with more of the process kept visible.

Payote frames its Made in France story around know-how, transparency, and local production in Perpignan. On its current page, the brand says its workshop designs quality espadrilles all year long and presents local fabrication as both a craft question and a commitment. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))

Atelier de Perpignan
Transparency first

Making the workshop part of the story, not the hidden backend.

Payote explicitly puts transparency at the center of this page, saying it shares the different stages “from the idea to the finished product” in order to build trust and signal quality. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))

In the redesign, that becomes the guiding logic of the page: French manufacturing is not treated as a vague label, but as a visible process tied to people, location, and production reality. This is an interpretation based on their stated emphasis on transparency and local making. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))

80% Of espadrilles sold in France are imported from abroad, according to Payote’s page. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))
The workshop

Perpignan as a place of production, not only a point of origin.

Payote states that all of its espadrilles are made in its workshop in Perpignan, and describes the process from cutting the canvas to gluing and stitching the label as work carried out by specialised artisans. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))

That matters because it shifts “Made in France” away from abstraction. The claim is anchored in a place, a workshop, and named production steps instead of a broad patriotic message alone. This framing is an inference from the page’s manufacturing description. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))

A label means little on its own. A workshop, a place, and visible hands make it credible.

The people

French manufacturing also means social conditions.

Their page says local making is not only about know-how. It also names fair pay, paid leave, a healthy and secure workplace, and decent living conditions as part of the argument for producing in France. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))

This is one of the strongest parts of the original page. It gives “Made in France” a human and social meaning, not just a commercial one. In the redesign, that idea is preserved but made easier to read as a core brand stance. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))

The ambition

Relocalisation as a direction, not a finished result.

Payote says that in 2024 it opened its first espadrille manufacturing workshop in Perpignan, describing it as a new step toward producing more and bringing espadrille know-how back to France. The page also says France currently produces around 800,000 pairs while about 4 million are sold each year in the country. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))

That makes the page stronger than a simple patriotic claim. It presents relocalisation as a long-term effort with a gap still to close, which gives the story more realism and more trust. ([payote.fr](https://payote.fr/pages/transparence))
